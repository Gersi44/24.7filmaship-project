A short biography

Alan Turing was born in 1912. He was educated at St-Michael's, a school that focused more on the classics than maths and science, the subjects that interested Turing the most. His brillant career begun when he was hired by the british governement during World War II as a cryptanalyst. Here he accomplished the first and most known of his numerous feats by inventing an electromechanical machine that could break the code used by the Enigma machine widely used by the Nazis. It is estimated that his work shortened the war by two to four years, therefore saving millions of lives. Turing was later arrested because of his homosexuality, and chose chemical treatments as an alternative to prison. He died of poisoning in 1954. His work was only recognized several years later because of its classified nature.
